## What is Image to Alpha Pattern?

Image to Alpha Pattern is a browser-based tool that takes any photo or graphic and transforms it into a **color-coded symbol matrix** — the kind used for alpha patterns in friendship bracelet making, cross-stitch, perler bead art, and similar crafts.

Upload your image, configure the grid size, tweak color thresholds, and the tool produces a numbered grid where each cell shows a letter symbol (A, B, C…) corresponding to a color in a palette legend. You can then export the result as a high-resolution PNG, a color-filled Excel workbook, a CSV, or a Google Sheets-compatible file.

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Upload an image">
    Drag a JPG, PNG, or WEBP file onto the upload zone — or click to browse your files. The tool reads the image in your browser; nothing is sent to a server.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure the matrix">
    Set the number of columns and rows for your grid. Enable aspect-ratio lock so the tool automatically adjusts the other dimension. Adjust the color-similarity and alpha thresholds to control palette size and edge treatment.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate the pattern">
    Click **Generate Pattern**. The image is processed by a Web Worker that averages pixel colors across each grid cell and assigns a letter symbol to every unique color.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Preview and edit">
    The interactive canvas shows your pattern at a glance. Zoom in with the scroll wheel, pan by dragging, or switch to the pencil tool to repaint individual cells. All edits are undoable.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Export">
    Download your pattern as a high-resolution PNG image, a color-filled Excel workbook, a plain CSV, a Google Sheets workbook, or a standalone HTML page.
  </Step>
</Steps>
